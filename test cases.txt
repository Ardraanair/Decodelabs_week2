case 1 Test the Chain-of-Thought Logic Engine using the following problem.

Problem: A bat and a ball cost ₹110 in total. The bat costs ₹100 more than the
ball. How much does the ball cost?

Solve according to the system instructions.

Reasoning Summary

1.  Fact Extraction: The total cost of the bat and ball is ₹110, and the price
    difference between them is ₹100.
2.  Step-by-Step Logic: By representing the cost of the ball as x, the cost of
    the bat is defined as x + 100. Setting up the equation x + (x + 100) = 110
    allows for the calculation of the unknown.
3.  Calculation: Solving the equation 2x + 100 = 110 results in 2x = 10, which
    simplifies to x = 5.

Final Answer

The ball costs ₹5.

case 2 If 5 machines make 5 widgets in 5 minutes,
how long would 100 machines take to make 100 widgets?

Reasoning Summary

1.  Fact Extraction: The initial rate is 5 machines producing 5 widgets in 5
    minutes, which implies each machine produces 1 widget every 5 minutes.
2.  Step-by-Step Logic: Since the machines work in parallel, increasing the
    number of machines to 100 to produce 100 widgets maintains the same ratio of
    one machine per widget.
3.  Calculation: Because each individual machine still takes 5 minutes to
    complete one widget, the total time for 100 machines to complete 100 widgets
    simultaneously remains unchanged.

Final Answer

It would take 5 minutes.

case 3 A patch of lily pads doubles in size every day. If it covers the entire lake
in 48 days, on what day was the lake half covered?

Reasoning Summary

1.  Fact Extraction: The lily pads double in size every day, and the lake is
    fully covered (100%) on Day 48.
2.  Step-by-Step Logic: Working backward from the point of full coverage, if the
    size doubles every day, it must have been half the size on the previous day.
3.  Calculation: Half of the total coverage (50%) occurs exactly one day before
    full coverage (100%), which is Day 48 minus 1.

Final Answer

The lake was half covered on Day 47.

case 5 A shirt costs ₹100. Its price is increased by 20% and then decreased by 20%.

What is the final price?

Reasoning Summary

1.  Fact Extraction: The initial price is ₹100. It undergoes a 20% increase
    followed by a 20% decrease on the new price.
2.  Step-by-Step Logic: Calculate the increased price by adding 20% of the
    original value. Then, calculate the decrease by taking 20% of the updated
    higher value, rather than the original ₹100.
3.  Calculation:
      - Increase: 100 + (0.20 \times 100) = 120.
      - Decrease: 120 - (0.20 \times 120) = 120 - 24 = 96.

Final Answer

The final price is ₹96.
